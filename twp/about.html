About Me & the Project

About the Project

This project started as a way for me to pay closer attention to how objects actually leave my life, rather than only noticing them once they have already become clutter. Objects we normally keep for a while end up being forgotten or even invisible until they finally get thrown away, and I wanted to make a more deliberate effort to notice that process as it happens. For example this week I noticed that I had a few items that I had been keeping for a while without realizing it, and that they were all in the same corner of my room. The more I thought about it, the more I realized that for me this project is really about noticing those small decisions and patterns of keeping, replacing, and postponing.

What interests me most is that objects do not always lose their meaning the moment they stop being useful. For example, the wrist wraps are a bit old but they're still in bad condition and I have a lot of memories attached to them, so I’m not ready to throw them away yet. However, I know deep down despite the lifts I've had with them that it's time to let them go, and that they will probably just sit in the corner of my room for a while until I finally get around to it. Some objects that are just decorative or sentimental can also be kept for a long time without being used, and I think that’s a really interesting aspect of material culture. Similar to the topic of material culture the cologne I have is mostly kept just because I like the look of the bottle and the scent, even though I haven’t used it in a while. It’s not really useful to me anymore, but it still has meaning as a decorative item that I like to look at.

In that way, this project has made me think more about ideas connected to material culture, especially the idea that everyday objects can hold personal and social meaning beyond their practical use. Discussion about material culture also anecdotally connects to a course im taking this semester about the history of sociology, where we talk about how material culture plays a large role in the world and I thought it was interesting enough to connect to my blog here. A large concept of material culture is the impact having material things has on our lives, and how they can shape our identity and social relationships. For example, the universal cat print is extremely unserious but as a material item I like hte culture of bein gunserious and just having fun with it, and I also have a lot of memories attached to it that make it meaningful to me. Similarly, the cologne I have is not just a scent, but it also represents my personal style and the memories associated with it. The idea of attaching and building your own culture to objects you own is so intersting to me so I made sure to include a few items that have a lot of personal meaning to me, even if they’re not really “trash” in the traditional sense.
