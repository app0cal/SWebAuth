Introduction

This page works as an inventory to note down important or interesting images related to bodybuilding that make up the everyday world of the topic. My most important goal is to provide a visual representation of the various aspects of bodybuilding. Instead of only defining bodybuilding through one object or one place, this page breaks it into two main parts: what happens in the gym, and what happens outside of the gym. Together, the two groups show that bodybuilding is not just about lifting weights, but also about habits, routines, planning, food, and discipline (but also having fun).

The first group, In the gym , focuses on the equipment, movements, and exercise spaces that directly shape training. These images help show what bodybuilding looks like physically and materially through machines, bars, dumbbells, benches, and other tools used during workouts. The second group, Out of the Gym, focuses on the less visible side of bodybuilding, such as dieting, meal preparation, supplements, water, sleep, and other everyday routines that support progress. These images matter because they show that bodybuilding is built just as much by what happens outside the gym as what happens inside it.

The intent with this organization is to show clean up two main misconceptions about bodybuilding. By organizing the page this way, the viewer can better understand bodybuilding as a whole system rather than just a collection of exercises. Having fun is one of the most important things you can do as a lifter to stay consistent, and the fact that you can have fun while building muscle is a key part of the bodybuilding experience. The page is meant to help viewers, novice or experienced, see how the topic is shaped by both visible training practices and quieter daily habits. In that way, it supports the overall purpose of the site by showing the broader world that bodybuilding depends on.
