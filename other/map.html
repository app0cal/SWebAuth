Gym Map

This map marks some of the main spaces in the gym that matter most for bodybuilding. The locations are split into two categories: training areas and support areas. Training areas are the places where the main lifts and exercises happen, while support areas are the places tied to tracking, recovery, or preparation. The markers link down to location profiles below, which explain why each spot matters and what kind of role it plays in the gym overall.

Location Profiles

Squat Rack

The squat rack is one of the main bodybuilding spaces in the gym because it is used for squats, overhead press, and other rack-based lifts.

  • Category: Training Area
  • Main use: Lower-body compounds
  • Typical data tracked: working weight, reps, sets
  • Importance: ★★★★★

Bench Area

The bench area is important for chest training and upper-body pressing work, especially bench press and incline variations.

  • Category: Training Area
  • Main use: Chest and pressing movements
  • Typical data tracked: bench PR, working sets, frequency
  • Importance: ★★★★★

Dumbbell Area

The dumbbell area supports a wide range of bodybuilding movements and is one of the most flexible spaces for accessory lifts.

  • Category: Training Area
  • Main use: Accessory and unilateral work
  • Typical data tracked: top set weight, rep range
  • Importance: ★★★★☆

Cable Machine

The cable machine is useful for more controlled bodybuilding movements and lets you target muscles from multiple angles.

  • Category: Training Area
  • Main use: Isolation work
  • Typical data tracked: pin setting, sets, reps
  • Importance: ★★★★☆

Cardio Area

The cardio area matters most during cutting phases when conditioning, calorie burn, and recovery planning start to matter more.

  • Category: Training Area
  • Main use: Conditioning and calorie expenditure
  • Typical data tracked: time, calories, intensity
  • Importance: ★★★☆☆

Scale

The scale is part of the support side of bodybuilding because it is tied to tracking body weight during bulks, cuts, and maintenance phases.

  • Category: Support / Utility Area
  • Main use: Tracking body weight
  • Typical data tracked: weight changes over time
  • Importance: ★★★★☆

Recovery / Utility Area

This area represents the side of the gym tied to preparation and recovery, such as stretching, regrouping between sets, or using support equipment.

  • Category: Support / Utility Area
  • Main use: Recovery and preparation
  • Typical data tracked: rest time, mobility work
  • Importance: ★★★☆☆
